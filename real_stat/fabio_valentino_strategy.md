# Fabio Valentino's Order Flow Scalping Strategy

**Source:** YouTube Video (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tvERE-Beu2U)

This strategy breakdown is based on the live trading session and interview with top-tier order flow scalper Fabio Valentino. The strategy relies heavily on Auction Market Theory, Volume Profile, and Order Flow (Footprint Charts/Cumulative Volume Delta) rather than traditional lagging indicators.

## 1. Market State & Context (The "Where")
Fabio determines the "narrative" of the market before taking any trades. He divides the market state into two conditions:
*   **Balance (Consolidation):** The market is rotating inside a Value Area. Transactions are efficient.
*   **Imbalance (Expansion/Trend):** The market breaks out of the Value Area, seeking new prices.

**Tools Used:**
*   **Volume Profile:** Tracks volume traded at specific price levels.
*   **Value Area (VA):** Where 70% of the volume is transacted.
*   **Point of Control (POC):** The single price level with the highest volume.
*   **Low Volume Nodes (LVN):** Price levels with very little volume, indicating quick rejections or gaps in liquidity. These act as strong reaction zones.
*   **Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD):** Measures aggressive buying vs. selling pressure in real-time.

## 2. Core Trading Models (The "How")

### Model 1: Value Area Reversion (The Consolidation Model)
Used when the market is in a balanced state (ranging).
*   **The Setup:** Price pushes out of the Value Area (fakeout/liquidation) and immediately snaps back inside.
*   **The Trigger:** Wait for the first aggressive push back into the range. Do *not* buy the exact bottom—wait for the first breakout of immediate structure (e.g., taking out the high of the selling candle). 
*   **The Confirmation:** Order flow must show aggressive sellers getting absorbed (big red orders punching a wall) and aggressive buyers stepping in. 
*   **The Target:** The POC (Point of Control) is the primary target. The opposite side of the Value Area is the secondary target.

### Model 2: Break & Retest of Low Volume Nodes (The Expansion Model)
Used when the market is trending heavily (imbalance).
*   **The Setup:** Price explodes out of a range, creating a large impulsive move. This usually leaves behind a Low Volume Node (LVN).
*   **The Trigger:** Wait for price to pull back into the LVN. 
*   **The Confirmation:** Wait for aggressive sellers to push into the LVN but fail to get follow-through. You need a **full 1-minute body candle close** above the local resistance to confirm buyers are back in control.
*   **The Target:** The previous structural high (Daily High, Session High) or trail the stop for massive asymmetrical R:R (1:5 to 1:10+).

## 3. Trade Execution & Risk Management (The "Edge")

Fabio's true edge lies in his intense risk management. He prioritizes protecting capital over win rate.

*   **Micro-Structural Stop Losses:** Instead of placing the stop loss vaguely "below the swing low," Fabio places it **1-2 ticks below the exact footprint bubble/order cluster** where the aggressive buyers stepped in. 
    *   *Why?* If that specific institutional order gets broken, the thesis is dead. He wants to be out immediately to avoid slippage during long liquidations.
*   **Lightning Break-Even ("Risk Zero"):** The moment price moves in his favor and breaks the immediate structure (e.g., clearing the next candle's high), he moves his stop loss to break-even. 
    *   *Why?* He avoids the mental stress of giving back profits. If it's a true squeeze, it shouldn't come back to his entry.
*   **Asymmetrical Risk vs. High Win Rate:** He frequently accepts small losses (e.g., $250) quickly. He can take 8-10 small stop-outs in a day because when the setup hits, it pays 1:4 to 1:5 (e.g., $1,000+).
*   **Daily Max Loss:** He risks 0.25% to 0.5% per trade. His daily max loss limit is strict (e.g., 2%). 
*   **House Money (Profit Compounding):** Once he secures a profit for the day (e.g., $1,000), he uses a fraction of *that specific profit* to fund his next, higher-risk trades. If he loses it, his account principal remains untouched. 

## 4. Key Takeaways
1. **Never Trade the Noise:** Don't engage in trades in the middle of chop unless it's from the extremes targeting the POC.
2. **Absorption is King:** Seeing massive aggressive orders (red/green footprint bubbles) that *fail to move price* is the strongest signal of a reversal.
3. **Be Fast to Cut:** If the trade doesn't explode into profit immediately upon entering a squeeze, the probability of failure increases dramatically. Cut the risk.
