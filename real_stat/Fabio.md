1. The Chart Setup (Engineering the Visuals for Precision)
During the hyper-volatile New York open (9:30 AM EST), traditional time-based charts are severely flawed. A massive battle between buyers and sellers can occur within a single 1-minute candle, masking the true data in a messy wick. Fabio engineers his charts to solve this:
40-Range Charts (Price/Distance over Time):
The Mechanic: A 40-Range chart prints a new candle only when the price has traveled exactly 40 ticks from high to low (e.g., 10 points on the Nasdaq). If the market goes completely flat for 5 minutes, no new candles print. If the market explodes, 10 candles might print in 10 seconds.
The Advantage: This standardizes volatility. It strips away "time noise" and reveals the raw structural swing points of the market. It allows Fabio to see clean breakouts and pullbacks regardless of how fast the market is moving.
Hollow Candles (Cognitive Efficiency):
By using hollow (empty) candles, the visual weight of the chart is reduced. Scalping requires split-second decisions; solid, brightly colored blocks of red and green can cause visual fatigue and emotional triggers. Hollow charts emphasize the wicks (where price was rejected) and the closes, which is all that matters for price action.
Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD) - The "Aggression" Tracker:
The Mechanic: Delta is calculated by taking the volume of aggressive market buys (hitting the Ask) and subtracting aggressive market sells (hitting the Bid). CVD tracks a running total of this metric throughout the session.
The Advantage (Divergence): Fabio uses CVD to spot hidden exhaustion. For example, if the price is making a new low, but the CVD is printing a higher low (meaning aggressive selling pressure has dried up), it warns him that a reversal is imminent.
2. Core Indicators: Volume Profile & Auction Market Theory
Volume Profile rotates the traditional volume bar from the bottom of the screen (time) to the side of the screen (price). It tells Fabio where the big money is doing business.
The Developing Value Area (VAH & VAL):
According to Auction Market Theory, the market spends 70% of its time at prices considered "fair value." The top boundary is the Value Area High (VAH) and the bottom is the Value Area Low (VAL).
Fabio monitors the Developing Profile, meaning these levels shift dynamically as the session progresses. If price is trading inside the Value Area, the market is balanced (choppy). If price breaks outside, the market is seeking new value (trending).
Point of Control (POC):
The single price level with the highest traded volume. This is institutional fair value.
The Mechanic: The POC acts as both a magnet (drawing price back toward it when the market ranges) and a brick wall (acting as heavy support/resistance when price tests it from the outside).
3. The Core Trade Setups ("The Models")
Fabio does not trade random patterns; he executes specific, highly asymmetrical setups where order flow confirms Volume Profile levels.
Model 1: The "Triple-A" Setup (Value Area Reversion / The 80% Rule)
The Theory: Statistically, if a market opens outside of yesterday’s or the current day's Value Area, but then crosses back inside the Value Area Low (VAL) and stays there, there is roughly an 80% chance it will rotate all the way to the other side (Value Area High).
The Execution: Fabio waits for the price to drop to the VAL. He doesn't just blindly buy; he watches the Order Flow. If he sees aggressive selling dry up and buyers step in, he buys the bounce.
The Target: His first target is the POC (the middle), and his final target is the VAH. This offers massive asymmetry: a risk of maybe 10 ticks for a reward of 50-100 ticks.
Model 2: The Absorption & Squeeze (Failed Auction / Trapped Traders)
The Setup: Fabio watches the tape (or footprint chart) at a key support level. He looks for massive red sell orders flooding the market, but notices the price stops moving down.
The Mechanic (Absorption): This means a massive institutional limit buy order (a passive "iceberg" order) is sitting on the bid, absorbing thousands of sell market orders.
The Trigger (The Squeeze): The aggressive sellers suddenly realize they are trapped. To exit a short, you must buy. Fabio executes his long entry the exact millisecond the trapped shorts begin buying to cover their positions, riding the violent upside "squeeze" fueled by their panic.
4. Execution & Position Management (Dynamic Scaling)
Amateurs buy their full position size at once. Fabio uses a military-like reconnaissance strategy to test the waters, keeping initial risk close to zero.
"Testing the Tape" (Starter Position):
He enters with a small fraction of his max size (e.g., 2–4 contracts). If he is wrong, the loss is a paper cut.
Pyramiding (Scaling in on Confirmation):
Unlike retail traders who "average down" into losing trades (increasing risk), Fabio "averages up." If his 2 contracts go into profit and the tape shows the squeeze is accelerating, he violently adds to the position (e.g., jumping to 10 or 20 contracts). He only risks large size when the market has already proven his thesis is correct.
Micro-Structural Stop Losses:
Standard advice says "put your stop below the previous swing low." Fabio puts his stop just 2 or 3 ticks below the actual order flow wall (the absorption level).
The Logic: If an institution was defending a price with a 5,000-lot buy order, and that level breaks, the dynamic of the market has fundamentally changed. The thesis is dead. He wants to be out immediately, rather than waiting for an arbitrary chart level to hit.
5. Dynamic Risk Management (The "Free Ride" Concept)
Fabio’s win-rate is completely secondary to his risk-to-reward ratio. His entire methodology is designed to fund his winners using zero-risk capital.
The Lightning Break-Even:
Scalping is about momentum. If a squeeze happens, it should happen fast. The moment the trade thrusts into profit, Fabio moves his stop-loss to his exact entry price. Even if the market flashes crashes 5 seconds later, he loses $0.
Manual Micro-Cuts (Reading the Tape):
He rarely lets the market hit his hard stop. If he enters a long position expecting buyers to aggressively lift the offer, but the tape suddenly stalls and large limit-sell orders appear above him, he will hit the "Flatten" button manually. He will take a $500 loss on a trade where he was willing to risk $2,000, saving $1,500 simply by recognizing the flow changed.
Trailing Profit via Market Structure:
As the 40-Range chart prints new legs up, he trails his stop below each new structural swing low. He is never trying to guess the exact top; he simply lets the market take him out when the trend bends.
6. Market Context & Psychological Mastery
The final pillar is Fabio's deep understanding of market state and his own psychological bandwidth.
Trading the Phase (Expansion vs. Contraction):
Contraction (Balance): When the market is chopping inside the Value Area, it is in price discovery. Fabio shifts to pure scalping—taking quick 10-15 tick profits and getting out.
Expansion (Imbalance): When the market breaks out of the Value Area, it is trending. This is the only time Fabio holds trades for massive moves, recognizing that the algorithms have shifted from "reversion" to "trend" mode.
The 30-Minute Opening Balance Rule:
The first 30 minutes of the NY Session (9:30 AM to 10:00 AM) are dominated by overnight position liquidations, algorithmic hedging, and retail emotion. Fabio watches this establish the "Initial Balance" (IB). He lets the amateurs blow their accounts in the chop, and then steps in after 10:00 AM to trade the true institutional direction established by the IB breakouts.
