# FX Alex G (SwingTradingLab) - The "Set & Forget" Trading Strategy

Based on the analysis of FX Alex G's YouTube channel (@fxalexg__), his course materials ("Swing Trading Lab"), and community discussions, here is a highly detailed and comprehensive breakdown of his proprietary "Set & Forget" trading strategy.

## 1. Core Trading Philosophy & Mindset
Alex G emphasizes psychology and discipline over complex indicators. His approach is designed to remove emotions, stress, and overtrading.

*   **The "Set & Forget" Methodology:** The cornerstone of his system. Once you conduct your analysis, set your entry limit orders (or execute at market with predefined levels), set your Stop Loss (SL), and set your Take Profit (TP), you step away from the charts. You let the edge play out without micromanaging or closing trades early out of fear.
*   **Weekly vs. Daily Goals:** Instead of forcing trades to meet a daily quota (e.g., $1,000/day), which leads to forcing bad setups, he advocates for a weekly mindset (e.g., aiming for $5,000/week). This forces the trader to wait for only the highest probability setups.
*   **The "Baseball Mindset":** Profitable traders are like batters waiting for the perfect pitch. You don't swing at every opportunity; you wait for your specific setup to form and strike once.
*   **Single Bias Trading:** To avoid confusion and over-analysis, stick to one bias (either strictly bullish or strictly bearish) based on the higher timeframe trend. Do not switch biases back and forth on the same day.
*   **Anti-FOMO:** Entering after big impulsive moves have already happened is a guaranteed losing strategy. You must wait for pullbacks into value areas.

## 2. Technical Framework & Chart Setup
Alex G keeps his charts exceptionally clean, relying almost entirely on pure price action and one moving average.

*   **The Only Indicator - 50 EMA (Exponential Moving Average):**
    *   Used to determine the dynamic trend direction.
    *   Acts as a dynamic support or resistance level.
    *   Price above the 50 EMA = Bullish bias. Price below the 50 EMA = Bearish bias.
*   **"No Gap" Candlesticks / Clean Charts:** He focuses heavily on the body and wicks of standard candlesticks to read price action without the noise of multiple overlapping indicators.

## 3. The Top-Down Analysis Process
The strategy relies heavily on multi-timeframe analysis to ensure you are trading in alignment with the macro trend.

*   **Step 1: The Macro Trend (Weekly & Daily Timeframes)**
    *   Identify the overall market structure (Higher Highs/Higher Lows for Uptrend; Lower Highs/Lower Lows for Downtrend).
    *   Determine the current bias.
*   **Step 2: Identifying Areas of Interest (AOI) (Daily & 4H Timeframes)**
    *   Draw horizontal zones (not just thin lines) at key Support and Resistance levels where price has historically reacted strongly.
    *   These are the *only* areas where you will look for trades. If price is in the "middle of nowhere," you do nothing.
*   **Step 3: The Pullback & The Execution Timeframe (1H & 15m Timeframes)**
    *   Wait patiently for the price to pull back into your predetermined AOI.
    *   **Crucial Alignment:** For a swing trade, the Weekly, Daily, and 4H should be in sync. If the Daily temporarily goes against the Weekly trend to reach a Weekly AOI, wait for the lower timeframes (4H/1H) to shift back into alignment with the macro trend before entering.

## 4. Entry Triggers & Confirmations
You do not enter *just* because price touches an AOI. You need confirmation.

*   **EMA Confluence:** The ideal setup occurs when price pulls back into a structural Area of Interest (AOI) *and* simultaneously touches or rejects the 50 EMA on the execution timeframe.
*   **Price Action Rejection:** Look for specific candlestick patterns rejecting the AOI/EMA confluence.
    *   *Bullish:* Pin bars with long lower wicks, bullish engulfing candles.
    *   *Bearish:* Pin bars with long upper wicks, bearish engulfing candles.
*   **The Setup Formula:** Trend Alignment + AOI Pullback + 50 EMA Touch + Candlestick Rejection = Valid Entry.

## 5. Risk Management & Trade Management (The "Forget" Phase)
This is where the strategy succeeds or fails.

*   **Asymmetrical Risk-to-Reward (R:R):** Always aim for a minimum of a **1:2 or greater** Risk-to-Reward ratio.
    *   If you risk 1% of your account, your Take Profit must yield at least 2%.
    *   *The Math:* With a 1:2 R:R, you can have a win rate of less than 40% and still be a profitable trader.
*   **Capital Preservation:** Risk a fixed, small percentage of your account per trade (strictly 1% to 2%). This ensures you can survive inevitable losing streaks.
*   **Stop Loss Placement:** Stop losses are placed objectively below the structure (below the wick of the rejection candle or below the entire AOI zone). Never move your stop loss further into the negative.
*   **Accepting the Loss:** Losses are a normal business expense. If the SL is hit, the trade is over. No revenge trading.

## Summary: The Perfect "Set & Forget" Trade Walkthrough
1.  **Sunday/Monday Prep:** Check the Weekly/Daily charts. Determine the macro trend. Are we making higher highs or lower lows?
2.  **Mark Your Zones:** On the 4H/Daily, draw rectangles around key Support/Resistance Areas of Interest (AOIs).
3.  **Wait:** Set price alerts near your AOIs. Do nothing until price reaches them.
4.  **Confirm (1H/15m):** Price enters your AOI. Is the 50 EMA also there? Does a 1H or 15m candle close showing a clear rejection (wick) of the zone?
5.  **Execute:** Enter the trade at the close of the confirmation candle.
6.  **Set & Forget:** Place your SL behind the zone/wick. Place your TP at the next logical structure level ensuring a minimum 1:2 R:R. Close your laptop and walk away.
